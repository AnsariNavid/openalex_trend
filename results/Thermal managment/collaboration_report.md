## Theme Summaries from Abstracts

### Computer science
Collaboration in computer science spans interdisciplinary efforts to address challenges such as energy consumption in computing infrastructure, integration of social sciences to tackle issues of equity and governance, and the development of carbon-responsive computing. Advances in quantum and high-performance computing are enabling breakthroughs in diverse fields, while machine learning and data management solutions are transforming manufacturing and scientific research. Educational innovations, such as dynamic e-books and peer assessment in group projects, are enhancing learning experiences and broadening participation in computing.

### Materials science
Materials science collaborations leverage computational chemistry, machine learning, and graph deep learning to accelerate the discovery and design of novel materials. Open-source tools and physics-inspired neural networks are enabling efficient modeling and prediction of material properties, even with limited data. The integration of large language models and advanced simulation techniques is streamlining data extraction and multiscale modeling, fostering reproducibility and innovation across chemical and materials research.

### Artificial intelligence
Collaborative efforts in artificial intelligence are driving advancements in healthcare diagnostics, medical data privacy, and drug discovery through deep learning, federated learning, and AI-driven pipelines. AI is also revolutionizing remote sensing, digital twin networks for wireless communications, and high-performance computing hardware. The field is addressing challenges in transparency, reproducibility, and energy efficiency, particularly as AI applications expand into new domains such as magnonics and non-charge-based information processing.

### Optoelectronics
Optoelectronics research is advancing through the development of novel materials, device architectures, and fabrication techniques for photodetectors, modulators, and IR devices. Innovations include controlled growth of nanoplates, flexible 2D material-based devices, and the use of orbital angular momentum of light to modulate device performance. These efforts are enabling high-performance, tunable, and integrable optoelectronic components for applications in communications, sensing, and next-generation photonic systems.

### Electronic engineering
Collaboration in electronic engineering focuses on overcoming the limitations of silicon technologies by exploring 2D materials for advanced transistors and circuits. Industry and academia are working together to develop scalable fabrication methods and optimize chip packaging processes, such as the integration of palladium layers for improved reliability and performance. These efforts are paving the way for beyond-CMOS devices and innovative microelectronics packaging solutions.

### Electrical engineering
Electrical engineering collaborations are at the forefront of developing magnonic circuits, smart manufacturing systems, and network architectures for AI deployment. The synergy between materials science, computer science, and electrical engineering is enabling energy-efficient, high-frequency data processing and robust industrial automation. Addressing the challenges of distributed AI model training and deployment over wireless networks is a key focus, aiming to ensure scalability, efficiency, and sustainability in future communication systems.
